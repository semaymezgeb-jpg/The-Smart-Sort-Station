Building a Cleaner, Greener Neighborhood, One Smart Bin at a Time

The Problem

In our surroundings, trash is typically disposed of in one pile, so plastic water bottles, food leftovers, and broken things all end up in one place. When these different wastes are all put together, they cannot be properly recycled, and most of them will burn or rot in the open areas. This is harmful to the environment and causes hazardous health risks, especially to people who live close to such dumping sites. Flies and stray animals are typically near piles of litter, and during rainy periods, it gets worse. There is also no easy system of waste segregation. We realized that many communities in Ethiopia, like ours, need affordable yet effective technology to facilitate the process of trash sorting.

Our Solution

Our solution is to build a smart trash sorting station using four simple electronics and sensors. The system's "brain" will be an Arduino Nano, a powerful, small, and affordable unit for this purpose. The system will be affordable and easy to use by anyone, even without much experience with technology. It will simplify the work of manual sorting and make it easy to gather clean, sorted garbage.

When you want to dispose of waste, you will simply press a button to choose the type of waste, and the respective lid will be opened.

Equipment List

Our smart sorting station is built with a range of electronic components that work together to identify and sort waste.

  • High-torque servo (MG996R/eqv.): A powerful motor that controls the gate to release the waste.
  • 5 V regulated supply & 5 V / 2 A buck converter: Power supply components to ensure the system runs smoothly.
  • Proximity sensors: We use a combination of inductive (for metal) and capacitive (for plastics and other dielectric materials) sensors to identify different materials.
  • Photoelectric/reflective sensor & Ultrasonic sensor (HC-SR04): Sensors used to detect and analyze the presence of objects.
  • IR break-beam pairs: Used to detect when an object passes a certain point in the system.
  • Load cell + HX711: Detects when an object is placed on the tray, initiating the sorting process.

About Us

Our Story and Mission

Our project was born from a simple observation in our community: trash is often disposed of in one pile, creating health and environmental hazards. This realization came to us while participating in the **Brighton Generation** course, which challenged us to observe our community and come up with a practical solution to a real-world problem. The course empowered us to turn this observation into a mission: to create an affordable and effective technology that facilitates trash sorting. Our goal is to provide a smart solution that simplifies waste management for everyone and promotes cleanliness in our environment, showing that a small idea can have a big impact.

Budget

To turn our vision into a reality, we have carefully planned our budget to ensure every contribution makes a direct impact. The total cost for the essential components of our ASTRA-Sort system is **11,370 ETB**. This includes the power supply, a range of advanced sensors, and the high-torque servo that makes our system work. The final project cost will depend on the specific type of load cell we use. Here is the full budget breakdown based on our options:
